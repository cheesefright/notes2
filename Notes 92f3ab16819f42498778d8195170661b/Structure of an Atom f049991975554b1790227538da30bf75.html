Reasons for the failure of the Bohr Model

  1. In the Bohr model, the e−e^-e−﻿ is assumed to follow certain stationary paths, but after De-Broglie Hypothesis and Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle it is confirmed that the position of electron can not be determined and is uncertain.
